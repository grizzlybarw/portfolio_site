GridX EmpowerRe-imagined

GridX had powerful energy APIs and a platform that was hard to put in front of real customers. This was a 2–5 year vision for fixing that — and making the future concrete enough for everyone to build toward it.

Role Lead Experience Designer
Team Design, Product, Engineering, Executives
Type Vision / Strategy
Domain Clean Energy · SaaS
The Problem
GridX had powerful energy APIs. What it didn't have was a front end that utilities could actually put in front of customers.

Utilities want to guide their customers through genuinely complex decisions — time-of-use rates, EV adoption, solar, rebates — without overwhelming them. GridX's Empower platform had the data, but was missing the embeddable front-end layer, the personalization tools, and the measurement capabilities utilities needed to deliver that guidance at scale.

The core problem: how do you help utilities get the right widget to the right customer at the right time — and prove it's working — while keeping implementation simple and on-brand for a hundred different utility clients?

This wasn't a ship-next-quarter project. It was a 2–5 year north star — a campaign-driven energy personalization system designed to get product, engineering, and executive stakeholders all pointing the same direction.

Process
01
Research
Synthesized qualitative research into problem statements and stress-tested them with internal teams. Two distinct user types emerged with very different needs. Utility users — program managers, marketers, admins — needed a simple way to orchestrate widget campaigns, target specific customer segments, and measure what was working. Utility customers needed clear, contextual guidance that made bills and rate options feel manageable, and surfaced next steps that actually led somewhere. I also locked product constraints upfront: white-labelable, API-forward, low-lift for utility IT. Those constraints shaped everything.
02
Hypothesis
If utilities can segment and sequence widgets like lightweight campaigns — and customers receive timely, contextual guidance — we'd see higher engagement and more program enrollments, with a lower support burden. That became the design brief that shaped everything downstream. Simple to state, surprisingly hard to design for.
03
Information Architecture
After going deep into GridX's API capabilities, I mapped the full information architecture for the reimagined platform. That 1,000-foot view revealed the natural structure of the product: a Campaign system for utilities to orchestrate widgets, an API explorer for education and custom widget creation, and a Widget Library — both pre-built and custom — as the seed of a future marketplace. Getting this structure right before wireframing prevented a lot of expensive rework later.
04
Wireframes
Moved quickly from lo-fi to hi-fi. The goal wasn't to validate aesthetics — it was to confirm that the right information was in the right place. The real deliverable was the prototype, so wireframes were a means to an end, not a destination.
05
Prototype
Built a complete high-fidelity prototype of the happy path to present to executives. Key surfaces included a Homepage with campaign insights and quick-start actions; a Campaigns section with filterable management and a creation wizard; Campaign Detail pages with embed code, reporting dashboards, and white-labeled widget previews; an APIs explorer for self-service education; and a Widget Library with pre-built and custom widget management — the foundation for a potential marketplace down the road.
Key Screens
Campaigns Dashboard
Campaign Insights surface active campaigns, impressions, and engagement at a glance — with a filterable list and one-click campaign creation.
Campaign Detail & Reporting
Each campaign surfaces embed code, live analytics dashboards, and a white-labeled widget preview — giving utilities everything to deploy and measure in one place.
Widget Library
A browsable library of pre-built widgets — Cost Over Period, Rate Comparison, EV Purchase, Solar Adoption — each tagged by category and API, with a path to custom widget creation.
Takeaways
Sometimes UX isn't about shipping tomorrow — it's about making the future concrete.

The vision was considered too forward-looking for immediate production. That was expected. It wasn't the goal. The goal was to give GridX leadership a shared, specific picture of where Empower could go — concrete enough to align around, detailed enough to actually inform roadmap decisions.

It worked. The exploration directly influenced enhancements that did ship: improved targeting, richer widget analytics, an expanded widget library. The demo sparked delivery work and set a practical direction forward, even if it's a long path.

Vision work is real design work. Done well, it changes how an organization thinks — not just what it builds next quarter. That's worth doing.
